Co(de)Factory

Co(de)factory is an installation that allows anybody to create art and become an artist. It empowers people by giving them the tools to create 3D forms, and offers the chance to have their digital piece fabricated in 3D and showcased in the Digital Revolutions exhibition at the Barbican.

For this piece, Karsten Schmidt has created a visual programming interface with simple design tools, which people can use to transform a single shape in countless ways to construct more complex objects. The interface is accessible to anybody online with a modern web browser (Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox), and all finished pieces can be uploaded to the online gallery of this website. Any existing object can be further edited, thus forming a chain of co-authorship.

The installation also includes a next generation, open-source 3D printer (an Ilios HD model) that will print select pieces, live in the gallery. Each day, the Barbican will curate the on-site showcase by choosing one piece from the online gallery to be printed and displayed. Day by day, piece by piece, the physical collection of art will grow.
